Future Work

Due to budgetary and time limitations, converting our proof of concept project into an independent multi-sensorial system could have been more feasible. However, the system that we have now can be expanded upon in future iterations of the project. Here are some ideas for future work:

Making the system more independent

The primary way this can be achieved is by loading the inference script and YOLO dependencies onto a mini-computer like a NUC that can also act as an edge device.

Weatherproofing

With an edge device connected to the Astrocam, the entire system can be housed in a mount with a transparent dome that provides basic weatherproofing.

Improving vision

Attaching lens, ie. a fisheye lens can increase the field of vision for drone detection. It should also require some de-warping as pre-processing. Additionally, adding another camera would allow stereovision that can be used to approximate the distance between the drone and the edge device.

Implementing secondary sensor

We tried implementing acoustic sensing but found out reliable detection would require expensive equipment that is well outside of our budget. It may be worthwhile exploring other avenues like infra-red or RF (without legal restrictions).

Improving training

To reduce the rate of false positives, constructing a custom dataset would be required. Preferably one built with footage featuring relevant drones in civilian spaces than mixing in military-related images that might not be ideal. Adding in negatives to offset the FP rate is crucial, we have tried 600 images for our 13k dataset - so that ratio should be increased. Updating the training framework to YOLOv8 might also be necessary.
